* These remarks are offered at the planning stage of a formal validation study.
Before that planning can usefully proceed, it is worth being explicit about
what validation is and is not. 

* Validation verifies the established performance of a finalized,
locked procedure - going into validation we must be confident
in the level of the procedure's performance.

* Having sound QC procedures tuned to the features of the 
test input data, reliable inclusion/exclusion criterion,
and a well undersrtood model selection process are necessary
to ensure that the test performance is stable and reproducible.

* The history of omics-based diagnostic development offers well-documented
examples of flawed products successfully meeting the reqirertmnrts 
of the validation runs, and making it into the clinical settings.
This is precisely why the NCI commissioned
the IOM review that produced the Evolution of Translational Omics guidelines.

* As an aside, it is not a well appreciatyed fact that statistical reasoning
is helpful in the analysis of data all along the data processing pipelines
of omic platforms.  I spend a significant amount of time making sure that
normalization works as expectred, and making sure that poor quality sample
are flagged to alert th downstream analysis modules.
Statistical input is not a validation-stage resource — it is a
development-stage requirement. The purpose of this document is to 
describe what needs to be in place before validation begins.
